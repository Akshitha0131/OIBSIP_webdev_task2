K.S. Chithra

The Nightingale of South India

Biography

K.S. Chithra, also known as the Nightingale of South India, is a renowned Indian playback singer and Carnatic musician. With a career spanning over four decades, she has recorded songs in multiple languages including Malayalam, Tamil, Telugu, Kannada, and Hindi. Her melodious voice and emotive singing have won her numerous awards, including six National Film Awards and several state awards. Chithra continues to inspire generations of music lovers around the world.

Achievements

  • 6 National Film Awards for Best Female Playback Singer
  • 8 Kerala State Film Awards
  • 25 Filmfare Awards South
  • Padma Shri by the Government of India in 2005
  • UNESCO Cultural Ambassador for South India

Popular Songs

  • "Kehna Hi Kya" (Bombay)
  • "Oru Deivam Thantha Poovae" (Kannathil Muthamittal)
  • "Poojaavaarayo" (Mozhi)
  • "Sundariye Vaa" (Malayalam)
  • "Chinna Chinna Aasai" (Roja)
